Aswan: A Tranquil Journey Through Egypt’s Timeless Beauty
By: Ali mehdi 14 Jan 2022
I just returned from an incredible trip to Aswan, and I’m still in awe of this peaceful, yet captivating city. Aswan feels like a world apart from the hustle and bustle of other Egyptian cities — it’s serene, breathtaking, and so rich in history. My journey started with a visit to Philae Temple, and wow, what a sight! Set on an island in the Nile, you have to take a short boat ride to reach it, and the moment I saw the temple rising out of the water, I knew I was in for something special. Philae is dedicated to the goddess Isis, and the intricate carvings and towering columns are a testament to ancient Egyptian craftsmanship. Walking through the temple complex, I couldn’t help but feel connected to the stories and legends that have been passed down for centuries. After Philae, I explored the famous Aswan High Dam, which is an impressive feat of modern engineering. It’s hard to believe that this dam has tamed the mighty Nile River, controlling its flow and providing electricity to millions. Standing there, overlooking the vast Lake Nasser, I felt like I was looking at the meeting point between ancient history and modern progress. One of the highlights of my trip was visiting the Nubian villages. The colors, the hospitality, the culture — everything about this experience was unforgettable. The Nubian people are so warm and welcoming, and their homes are painted in vibrant colors that reflect the spirit of their community. I had the chance to enjoy some traditional Nubian food, which was delicious, and learned more about their unique customs and traditions. This visit gave me a glimpse into a side of Egypt that’s often overlooked but deeply fascinating. Aswan is also home to the incredible Abu Simbel temples, which I made sure to visit. These colossal statues of Ramses II are carved directly into the mountainside, and they’re beyond impressive. The precision and scale of these temples are mind-blowing, and learning about how they were moved piece by piece to avoid being flooded by Lake Nasser was equally astonishing. Standing in front of the massive statues, with the desert stretching out behind them, I could really sense the grandeur of ancient Egyptian civilization. But it wasn’t all history and temples — one of the most relaxing parts of my trip was sailing along the Nile in a felucca. The gentle sway of the boat, the sound of the water lapping against the sides, and the sight of the sun setting over the palm-lined banks was pure tranquility. It’s the perfect way to take in the natural beauty of Aswan and let the peaceful atmosphere wash over you. Aswan is truly a hidden gem. It’s quieter and less crowded than other Egyptian cities, but it has so much to offer. From ancient temples to stunning landscapes, and the warm hospitality of its people, this city has left a lasting mark on me. It’s a place where you can slow down, soak in the history, and experience the true heart of Egypt. I can’t wait to return to this beautiful, timeless corner of the world.
